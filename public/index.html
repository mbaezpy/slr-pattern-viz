Crowd-provided exclusion patterns

In this page we illustrate the results from a crowdsourcing task run in Crowdflower, where we asked workers to classify abstracts as included / excluded based on a given inclusion criteria, and to provide us the exceprt from the abstract that informed their decision (the pattern). Below we illustrate the task description.

Results

We collected 2 (trusted) judgments for each of the 20 papers in our dataset. Those containing more judgments are the golden data (identified by the icon ). Those who did not provided a valid pattern (with no match in the abstract) are considered invalid. Most of the invalid cases relate to patterns provided in Spanish.

Below, the patterns are highlighted in the abstracts using the intensity of the marker to denote their popularity. Each pattern is tagged with the number of workers who provided it ( 1Pattern. You can click on the counter at start of the pattern (1) to focus the list on the judgements provided by those workers who highlighted that pattern.
